## 1. Abstract

Education in India today faces a silent crisis. Primary and Secondary school students (Class 1 to 10) have access to more content than ever, yet genuine understanding remains rare. Most EdTech platforms flood students with answers, leaving no room for curiosity, reasoning, or real learning. The result is a generation that can search but cannot think.

**Clarix** is an AI-powered Socratic tutoring platform designed specifically for Indian school students from Class 1 to 10, supporting State Board, CBSE, and ICSE curricula. Rather than delivering direct answers, it acts as a personal AI tutor that understands the student's textbook context, guides them through concepts using interactive explanations, and then reinforces understanding through MCQs and Flashcards — ensuring students arrive at answers themselves.

The platform features:
- Immersive 3D interface
- Age-adaptive AI responses (handwriting-style animations for younger students, faster responses for seniors)
- Gamified learning progression
- Voice input support
- Parent dashboard for tracking weak and strong areas
- Student memory that adapts teaching style over time

Built on React, Spline, Node.js, Appwrite, and Supabase with RAG-based textbook understanding powered through N8N AI workflows — Clarix is not just another EdTech tool. It is a digital Guru that thinks before it answers, because **real learning begins with the right question, not the right answer.**

## 2. Problem Statement

India has over **250 million school-going students**, with Maharashtra alone accounting for **20+ million Primary and Secondary students (Class 1–10)**. Despite the EdTech boom post-COVID, the core problem remains unsolved — students are getting answers, not understanding.

### The Real Problems

**1. Answer-First Culture**
Google, YouTube, and most AI tools hand students direct answers instantly — killing curiosity, critical thinking, and long-term retention.

**2. No Textbook Context**
No existing AI tool knows what textbook the student has open, which chapter they are reading, or what board they follow. Explanations remain generic and disconnected from actual curriculum.

**3. Same Experience for Everyone**
A Class 2 student and a Class 9 student get identical interactions on most platforms. Age-appropriate, grade-sensitive learning simply does not exist.

**4. No Memory, No Personalization**
Every session starts from zero. Platforms don't remember a student's weak topics, past struggles, or learning patterns — making every interaction shallow.

**5. Parents Are Left Out**
Parents have no real visibility into their child's actual understanding beyond exam results.

## 8. Proposed Solution

### What We Are Building

An AI-powered web platform where students from Class 1 to 10 get a personal tutor that knows their textbook, understands their level, and guides them to answers rather than giving them away.

### How It Works

**Step 1 — Student Signs Up**
Student registers with their class, board and school details. The platform personalizes itself instantly based on this information.

**Step 2 — Student Opens a Textbook**
The platform loads the exact textbook aligned to the student's board and class. The AI automatically gains context of the open book and chapter.

**Step 3 — Student Asks a Doubt**
Instead of getting a direct answer, the AI explains the concept, retells the context from the textbook and then presents MCQs or Flashcards to help the student arrive at the answer themselves. Includes age-adaptive responses (handwriting-style animations for younger students, faster responses for seniors).

**Step 4 — AI Learns and Adapts**
After every session the AI remembers weak topics, tracks progress and adjusts difficulty and explanation style for the next session.

**Step 5 — Parent Checks Dashboard**
Parents can log in separately to view their child's progress, strong topics and areas that need attention.

### Key Components

| Component | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Spline 3D Interface | Immersive landing and UI experience |
| Student Auth | Class, board and profile based personalization |
| Textbook RAG Pipeline | AI reads and understands the actual textbook content |
| AI Tutor Engine | Socratic responses, MCQs and Flashcard generation |
| Memory Layer | Tracks student struggles and adapts over time |
| Parent Dashboard | Simple progress and performance visibility |

## 11. Show Stoppers & Risk Analysis

**Technical Risks**
Building a real-time RAG pipeline that accurately reads and responds from textbook content within a hackathon timeline is challenging. API latency and AI response accuracy need to be consistently maintained for a smooth user experience.

**Regulatory / Compliance Risks**
Digitizing and using school textbook content for AI training may raise copyright concerns with publishers and boards. We will use only government published open-access textbooks like NCERT to stay compliant.

**Scalability Challenges**
Handling thousands of concurrent student sessions with real-time AI responses and vector search will require careful infrastructure planning. The current MVP is designed for demo scale with production scaling as a next phase.

**Future Uncertainties**
AI models evolve rapidly and responses may need continuous fine-tuning to stay curriculum accurate. Gaining trust and adoption from schools, parents and educational institutions will take time and consistent proof of results.
